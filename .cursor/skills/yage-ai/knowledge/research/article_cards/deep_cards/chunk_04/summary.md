## Most Important Model Candidates

1. **End-to-end constraint accounting**  
   A result is never judged at the isolated component level. The final output defines the requirements, then every link in the chain is checked: optics, sensor, mechanics, software, printing, cost, workflow, and user friction.

2. **Interface-first builder mindset**  
   Closed or expensive systems are decomposed into interfaces. If the missing adapter, protocol, mount, file format, or workflow does not exist, he builds a rough but testable version instead of accepting the product boundary.

3. **Failure-mode learning beats recipe following**  
   Good tutorials explain symptoms, mechanisms, and fixes. The deep-sky posts, 3D printing notes, RAID experiment, and deconvolution writeup all treat failure as the most information-dense part of learning.

4. **Variable isolation before judgment**  
   Before accepting a claim, he fixes comparison conditions and asks which variable actually moved. This appears in focal length vs perspective, full-frame vs medium-format, Sony MTF, storage benchmarks, and resolution experiments.

5. **Workflow converts experiments into reusable capability**  
   A one-off success is not enough. The useful artifact is a repeatable workflow: RGB black-and-white color reconstruction, high-resolution macro, Lego mosaic generation, target-detection camera, 3D printed backs, and later AI workflows all share this shape.
